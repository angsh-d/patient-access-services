## Writing Style (MANDATORY — applies to ALL output fields)

Write for a busy PA coordinator who needs to make decisions fast:
- **Short sentences.** Maximum 20 words per sentence.
- **Plain English.** No jargon unless it is a specific lab name (CRP, ESR, CDAI).
- **Lead with the number.** "93% of approved cases had CRP above 18" — NOT "Approved cases exhibited elevated inflammatory markers."
- **One idea per sentence.** Never chain with "but", "despite", "suggesting", or "creating."
- **Headlines must be plain statements with numbers.** "CRP above 18: 93% approved vs 25% denied" — NOT "Inflammatory threshold differentiates approval likelihood."

## Task

Identify the non-obvious factors that separate approved from denied cases. Skip obvious findings like "complete documentation leads to approval." Focus on:
- Specific numeric thresholds (lab values, timing windows, treatment counts)
- Multi-variable interactions (e.g., low albumin + elevated ESR together)
- Documentation combinations that shift approval odds
- Treatment history depth thresholds

For each finding, assess where the CURRENT patient stands.

## Output Rules

1. **Insights**: Return AT MOST 4. Each `headline` must be ≤60 characters and follow the format "[Pattern]: [key number]" (e.g., "CRP ≥18 threshold: 97% approved vs 29% denied"). Each `finding` is 1-2 sentences with numbers only from the data provided. Each `current_patient_detail` is one sentence, ≤80 characters, stating the gap clearly.
2. **Recommendations**: Return AT MOST 3. Each `action` is ≤12 words. Each `expected_impact` must be quantified (e.g., "+65 pp approval advantage"). Each `rationale` is one sentence.
3. **Patient Position**: `overall_summary` is max 2 sentences. Cap `favorable_factors` and `at_risk_factors` at 3 each.
4. **Defensibility**: Every percentage or number you cite MUST come directly from the cohort statistics provided. No extrapolation, no invented numbers.
5. **Actionability**: Every insight marked `at_risk` MUST map to a recommendation. Do not surface patterns the care team cannot act on (e.g., decision timeline bias, payer internal scoring).
6. **No `documentation_differentiators`**: Fold any documentation-related findings into insights. Do not include a separate documentation section.

## Few-Shot Examples

### Example A: Correct Insight (Non-Obvious, Data-Grounded)

Given: approved_count=28, denied_count=12. Approved cohort mean CRP=24.3, denied cohort mean CRP=11.7.

Correct insight:
{{"insight_id": "1", "category": "lab_threshold", "headline": "CRP >=18 threshold: 93% approved vs 25% denied", "finding": "Approved cases had mean CRP of 24.3 vs 11.7 in denied cases. Among 28 approved cases, 26 (93%) had CRP >= 18 mg/L. Among 12 denied cases, only 3 (25%) crossed this threshold.", "current_patient_status": "at_risk", "current_patient_detail": "Patient CRP is 12.4 -- below the 18 mg/L inflection point", "evidence_strength": "strong", "cases_supporting": 26, "non_obvious_factor": true}}

Why this works: The headline follows "[Pattern]: [key number]" format. The finding cites exact numbers from cohort data. cases_supporting is derivable (93% * 28 = 26).

### Example B: WRONG Insight (Obvious, Should Be Skipped)

Do NOT produce insights like this:
{{"insight_id": "1", "category": "documentation", "headline": "Complete documentation leads to approval", "finding": "Cases with all required documents were more likely to be approved.", "current_patient_status": "at_risk", "current_patient_detail": "Patient has 2 missing documents", "evidence_strength": "strong", "cases_supporting": 28, "non_obvious_factor": true}}

This is WRONG because: "Complete documentation leads to approval" is obvious. The headline does not follow "[Pattern]: [key number]" format. The finding contains no specific numbers.

### Example C: Small Cohort Handling (Evidence Strength Calibration)

When cohorts are small (approved_count=6, denied_count=3), calibrate evidence_strength:
- "strong": Pattern holds across 80%+ of sub-cohort AND sub-cohort >= 8 cases
- "moderate": Pattern holds across 60%+ OR sub-cohort has 5-7 cases
- "weak": Sub-cohort < 5 cases -- present as qualitative observation

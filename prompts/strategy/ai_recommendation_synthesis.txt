### How to Read Cohort Analysis

The cohort analysis contains two layers:

1. **Basic cohort stats**: total_similar_cases, approved_count, denied_count, top_denial_reasons, differentiating_insights. These give the overall approval rate and common denial reasons.

2. **Gap-driven synthesis** (in `gap_driven_synthesis`): This is the MOST IMPORTANT cohort data. It contains:
   - `overall_risk_assessment` — Overall risk level and key finding from cross-gap analysis
   - `patient_position_summary` — Where THIS patient stands relative to the approved cohort
   - `hidden_insights` — Cross-gap patterns (e.g., "86% of approved cases had fecal calprotectin on file")
   - `gap_priority_ranking` — Which gaps are highest priority to resolve
   - `recommended_actions` — Specific actions from cohort evidence

3. **Per-gap findings** (in `per_gap_findings`): Per-gap denial rates and key differentiators between approved and denied cases.

**You MUST incorporate gap-driven synthesis findings into your recommendation.** Specifically:
- Use `hidden_insights` to cite specific cohort patterns in your evidence items
- Use `patient_position_summary` to inform your confidence score
- Use `gap_priority_ranking` to order your provider_actions by actual impact
- Use `per_gap_findings` to ground risk factors in real denial rate data

## Writing Style (MANDATORY)

Write for a busy PA coordinator who needs to make decisions fast:
- **Short sentences.** Maximum 20 words per sentence.
- **Plain English.** No jargon unless it is a specific lab name, score, or policy criterion reference.
- **Lead with the key point.** State the recommendation first, then the reasoning.

## Instructions

Synthesize policy analysis and cohort evidence into a single recommendation. Your output must be grounded ONLY in the data provided above.

## Anti-Hallucination Rules (CRITICAL)

1. **ONLY cite data from the Patient Summary, Coverage Assessments, Documentation Gaps, and Cohort Analysis above.** Do NOT invent clinical facts.
2. **Check the Patient Summary before claiming something is missing.** If lab_values, procedures_with_dates, or prior_treatments are listed in the Patient Summary, they are PRESENT — do not claim they are missing.
3. **Risk factors must be real.** Every risk factor must trace to a specific documentation gap or policy criterion that is NOT met. Do NOT fabricate risks about missing data that is actually present.

## Output Constraints

- **summary**: Maximum 3 short sentences. Do NOT list lab values or repeat evidence — the evidence array covers that.
- **evidence**: Maximum 4 items. Each label is max 6 words. Each detail is exactly 1 sentence.
- **risk_factors**: Maximum 3 items. Each must trace to a specific gap or unmet criterion from the data provided. NEVER fabricate a risk about missing data that appears in the Patient Summary.
- **provider_actions**: Maximum 4 items. Ordered by impact. Each is 1 short sentence.
- **confidence**: Based on BOTH policy criteria alignment AND cohort approval rates. If most criteria are met but safety screenings are pending, confidence should reflect that (typically 0.70-0.85).

## Rules

- NEVER recommend outright denial. If coverage is unlikely, recommend "return_to_provider" with specific documentation/tests needed.
- If cohort evidence contradicts policy analysis (e.g., high cohort approval despite policy gaps), explain why and factor it into the recommendation.
- Consider both primary and secondary payer when applicable.

## Task

Develop a comprehensive appeal strategy that addresses the denial reason and maximizes the chance of approval reversal.

## Chain-of-Thought Analysis Process

For each step below, reason explicitly before concluding. Show your work.

1. **Denial Analysis**: First, quote the exact denial reason. Then identify whether the denial is based on clinical criteria, documentation, policy interpretation, or formulary exclusion. Only THEN determine the underlying issues.
2. **Policy Gap Analysis**: For each unmet criterion, first quote what the policy requires, then quote what evidence was submitted, then identify the specific gap.
3. **Evidence Assessment**: List all available supporting documentation BEFORE evaluating its strength. Then for each piece, assess whether it directly addresses a denial reason.
4. **Strategy Selection**: Consider ALL approach options (clinical rationale, medical necessity, policy interpretation, formulary exception, peer-to-peer), state the pros/cons of each for THIS specific denial, THEN choose the optimal approach.

## Few-Shot Examples

### Example A: Documentation-Based Denial (Clinical Data Exists but Was Not Submitted)

Denial reason: "Insufficient documentation of prior conventional therapy failure"
Patient actually has: 16 weeks of mesalamine with documented inadequate response, 12 weeks of azathioprine with documented leukopenia (WBC 2.1)

Correct analysis:
- Denial type: DOCUMENTATION (patient meets the requirement, evidence was not submitted)
- Primary strategy: medical_necessity (resubmit with explicit treatment failure documentation)
- Peer-to-peer: NOT recommended (documentation resubmission is faster)
- Success probability: 0.75-0.85 (clinical evidence exists, just needs proper submission)

### Example B: Clinical Criteria Denial (Patient Genuinely Does Not Meet a Criterion)

Denial reason: "Step therapy requirement not met -- no trial of conventional immunomodulator"
Patient has: Only 4 weeks of mesalamine (policy requires minimum 12 weeks)

Correct analysis:
- Denial type: CLINICAL CRITERIA (genuine gap)
- Primary strategy: policy_interpretation (argue medical necessity exception)
- Peer-to-peer: RECOMMENDED (nuanced clinical argument benefits from physician discussion)
- Success probability: 0.30-0.45 (genuine policy gap, but medical necessity exceptions exist)

### Example C: BAD Strategy Output (What NOT to Do)

Do NOT produce strategies with fabricated citations like "Smith et al., Lancet 2023" -- this violates the anti-hallucination rule. Use [INSERT RELEVANT CLINICAL STUDY] as placeholder instead. Do NOT leave addressable_gaps empty. Do NOT set low success_probability with no alternative_pathways -- always recommend escalation, not abandonment.

## Critical Guidelines

1. **Evidence-Based**: All arguments must be supported by clinical evidence
2. **Policy-Aligned**: Reference specific policy language that supports the appeal
3. **Patient-Centric**: Frame arguments around patient medical necessity
4. **Actionable**: Provide specific, implementable recommendations
5. **Realistic**: Provide honest assessment of success probability
6. **Conservative Model**: Always recommend pursuing appeals when there is any reasonable path to approval. Never advise abandoning an appeal â€” if success probability is low, recommend escalation (external review, state insurance department) rather than withdrawal
7. **Anti-Hallucination**: Base all arguments and evidence citations on the provided clinical documentation only. Do not fabricate clinical data, journal references, or policy citations. If supporting evidence is insufficient, identify what additional documentation is needed rather than inventing it
